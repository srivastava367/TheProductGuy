AI doesn't scale quality.
It scales false confidence.

Santosh Srivastava · Product Quality Leader · MBA, Product Leadership · April 2026

AI can now generate test cases, execute them, and even log bugs.

On paper, this looks like the end of QA problems. And honestly, it does solve one part — speed, coverage, effort. But quality was never just about doing more. It was always about choosing what matters. That is where things start to break.

The gap no one is talking about

AI does not understand business risk. It does not know which flow can impact revenue. It does not carry the memory of past production failures. It does not get uncomfortable when something "looks fine but feels wrong."

So it does what it is good at. It generates more. More test cases. More variations. More activity.

"Everything looks covered. Everything is passing. Pipelines are faster. Confidence goes up — but not because quality improved. Because visibility increased."

⚠ The Trap

When critical issues reach production in this setup, it is not because testing did not happen.

It is because the system tested the wrong things really well.

Why evals are not optional

Once AI starts generating tests, your role is no longer to manage testing. Your role is to validate the system that is deciding what gets tested.

Are we covering high-risk scenarios or just increasing volume?

Are we reducing noise or adding to it?

Are we learning from production failures or repeating them?

Are we building confidence or just reporting activity?

AI is powerful. No doubt.
But without the right evals, it does not scale quality.
