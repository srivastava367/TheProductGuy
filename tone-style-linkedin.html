Generative AI and Writing / 2026

Most people pick a topic to write about.
The sharp ones also pick a tone.

Tone and style are not decoration. They are the difference between a prompt that gets a generic answer and one that gets something you would actually use. Here is the mental model I wish someone handed me earlier.

The Oxford English Dictionary does not have a fixed list of tones. That is not a gap in the dictionary. That is the point. Tone is descriptive, not prescriptive. New ones emerge with culture (think "AI-native" or "meme-driven"), and styles shift across domains constantly.

But for practical writing and prompting, you need a working set. Clean, categorized, usable. Here it is.

The practical truth

You do not pick a tone. You do not pick a style. You combine them and that combination is where your voice lives.

Part 3   Combinations in the wild

Three examples of how real writing and prompting actually stacks these:

For a LinkedIn post
Reflective + Encouraging
Storytelling + Concise + Hook-driven
For a technical explainer
Neutral + Matter-of-fact

Energetic + Optimistic
Hook-driven + Data-backed + Concise
For a contrarian take
Assertive + Ironic
Contrarian + Framework-based + Conversational

Next time you write a prompt or a post, do not just describe what you want to say. Tell the tool, or yourself, exactly how it should feel to read it.

Try this now: Pick one tone from above. Pick two style attributes. Write your next prompt with those three words before your actual request. The output will surprise you.
