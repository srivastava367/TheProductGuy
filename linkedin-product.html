Most people on LinkedIn are working extremely hard — and getting very little back. More posts. Better captions. Trending hashtags. Engagement pods. The effort is real. The results, often, are not. And I think I know exactly why.
The Problem

Everyone Is Optimising
for the Wrong Thing

Studying product frameworks during my MBA at the Institute of Product Leadership gave me a lens I didn't expect to apply here. But once I saw it, I couldn't unsee it.

Most people treat LinkedIn like a platform they show up on. But LinkedIn behaves like a product — one that needs to be built, distributed, and grown with deliberate intention.

And the overwhelming majority are only doing one of the three.

Posting without a distribution or retention strategy is like shipping product features into a void — with no users, no feedback, and no growth loop.

The Framework

Your LinkedIn Is
a Product. Map It That Way.

If you apply basic product thinking, the entire LinkedIn puzzle snaps into place. Three pillars. Each one non-negotiable.

📦
Pillar 01
Content = Features

Features exist to create value for users. If your content doesn't educate, challenge, or shift perspective — people won't return. No retention. No growth. Weak features mean churn, and churn means a dead product.

📡
Pillar 02
Network = Distribution

Even the best product fails without a go-to-market strategy. Your network is your distribution channel. Posting without building distribution is like launching to an empty room and wondering why nobody showed up.

💬
Pillar 03
Engagement = Retention

Comments, replies, DMs, genuine conversations — this is your repeat usage metric. Retention is what separates products people love from products they forget existed. It's also the signal the algorithm needs to amplify you.

· · ·
The Diagnosis

Where Most People's
Loop Is Broken

The typical LinkedIn advice is: "Just post consistently."

That's the equivalent of telling a Product Manager to keep shipping features — while ignoring acquisition and retention entirely. It doesn't work in product. It doesn't work on LinkedIn either.

The people struggling on LinkedIn aren't struggling because their content is bad. They're struggling because:

  • ✕
    Broken Distribution They're distributing to the wrong people — or to no one with any intention at all.
  • ✕
    Zero Retention Effort They're not engaging — so the algorithm has no signal to amplify their content.
  • ✕
    Broadcast Mindset They're treating LinkedIn as a megaphone, not a relationship engine.

In Practice

What This Looks Like
Day to Day

The shift isn't about doing more. It's about doing the right things in the right sequence, with the right mindset. Here's how the framework translates to action:

  • ✓
    Build — Create with a point of view Not just observations. Take a stance. Challenge assumptions. Make someone think differently.
  • ✓
    Distribute — Connect with intent Your network is a channel. Curate it. Connect with people you genuinely want to learn from and contribute to.
  • ✓
    Engage — Show up before expecting others to Be present in others' conversations. Comments are currency. Don't be broke.
  • ✓
    Repeat — Treat this as a system, not a campaign Iterate. Improve. A product is never "done." Neither is your LinkedIn presence.

Stop asking "Why isn't my content performing?" Start asking "Where is my growth loop breaking?" That's a product manager's question — and it gets product manager results.
